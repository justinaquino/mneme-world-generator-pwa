## Earth-Centric Definitions

Mneme acknowledges Earth-centric terminology doesn't apply universally.
- "Ice" means frozen volatiles — not necessarily water ice
- "Ocean" is any large continuous liquid body
- Terms align with astronomical science with notable variations

---

## Stellar Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Star** | "A luminous spheroid of plasma held together by its own gravity." Sol is the nearest star and the measurement standard. |
| **Companion Star** | Orbits the system's central star. ~1/3 of stars hosting solar systems have companion stars. |
| **Giant Stars** | Stars 10× brighter than Sol. Larger and rarer; excluded from current Mneme generation rules. |
| **Sol** | Mass = 1.989 × 10³⁰ kg. Other stars measured in Solar Mass (SM) and Solar Luminosity (SL). |
| **Solar Luminosity** | L☉ = SCM × SM (Stellar Class Modifier × Solar Mass). Greater luminosity extends all zones. |
| **Sun** | "The Primary Star of a Solar System" (not necessarily Earth's Sun). |
| **Solar System** | "The system of celestial bodies that form around a star." |
| **Binary Star System** | Solar system with 2 stars; most massive = primary. |

## Circumstellar Structures

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Circumstellar Disc** | Discs of gas, dust, and planetoids orbiting a star. Young solar systems form these before planets coalesce. |
| **Frost Line** | Distance where water mostly freezes and stays frozen. Marks Inner/Outer Solar System boundary. |
| **Hot Zone** | From outer Infernal Zone limit to √L☉ × 0.8 AU. Also called Greenhouse Zone. |
| **Infernal Zone** | Too close to star for gaseous compounds to be retained. Extends from surface to Infernal Limit. |
| **Infernal Limit** | √Luminosity × 0.4 AU from the star. |
| **Tidally Locked** | Worlds in Infernal Zone — tidally locked on a 2D6 roll > 4. |

## Habitable Zone Definitions

| Zone | Distance from Star |
|------|--------------------|
| Infernal Zone | 0 AU to √L☉ × 0.4 AU |
| Hot Zone / Optimistic HZ | √L☉ × 0.4 AU to √L☉ × 0.8 AU |
| Conservative HZ | √L☉ × 0.8 AU to √L☉ × 1.2 AU |
| Cold Zone / Optimistic HZ | √L☉ × 1.2 AU to √L☉ × 4.85 AU |
| Outer Solar System | √L☉ × 4.85 AU onward |

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Conservative Habitable Zone** | Like Earth's orbit. Range: 0.8–1.2 AU from star. |
| **Optimistic Habitable Zone** | From atmosphere-stripping limit to where water stays frozen. Range: 0.4–4.85 AU. |
| **Cold Zone** | Mneme term. Between Conservative HZ and Frost Line. |
| **Outer Solar System** | Beyond Frost Line; volatiles remain frozen without greenhouse atmospheres. |

## Planetary Systems

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Planetary System** | Exclusively non-stellar celestial objects (excludes stars, dust, spacecraft, debris). |
| **Stellar System / Multi-star System** | 3+ star systems only. |

Sol Circumstellar Disk examples:
- Asteroid Belt: 2.1–3.25 AU, ~3% Luna mass
- Kuiper Belt: 30–60 AU, ~160% Luna mass

Ring masses:
- Saturn: 1.54×10¹⁹ kg (2% Ceres mass)
- Jupiter: ~10¹¹ kg
- Uranus: ~10¹⁶ kg
- Neptune: ~4.4×10¹⁶ kg

## Gas World Classifications

| Class | Name | Primary Compound | Temp | Position | Examples |
|-------|------|-----------------|------|----------|---------|
| I | Ammonia Cloud | Ammonia | <150 K | Beyond frost line or near cool stars | Jupiter, Saturn |
| II | Water Clouds | Water vapor | 250–700 K | Closer to star | HD 28185 b, Gliese 876 b |
| III | Cloudless | Methane dominant | 350–800 K | — | HD 37124 b, 55 Cancri c |
| IV | Alkali Metal | CO dominant | 900–1300 K | Hot Zones | 55 Cancri b, HD 209458 b |
| V | Silicate Cloud | Silicate/iron | ≥1400 K | Hot Zone | Kepler-7b, 51 Pegasi b |

**Ice World:** Mainly composed of frozen solid compounds heavier than H/He but lighter than silica/iron. Neptune (17 EM) and Uranus (14 EM) are Ice Giants.

**Ocean World:** Ice world warmed enough to possess significant liquid compounds. Liquid methane exists to √(1 SL) × 7 AU.
